title: For Quyn 
---

> [!note]
> This is a random thought that came to me. I dont even know if this makes sense or not...

I have never met her, I have never seen her face or heard her voice in real life, never accidentally passed by each other on the street. We live far away from each other, in different area. Somehow, I feel a connection with her. From my perspective, she is a quiet girl, but full of empathy. She has a soft voice, long hair, and smiling eyes. Sometimes, I imagine her just sitting there, quietly listening and comforting me with a few words. Even though we've never met, her presence feels warm and familiar, like a friend who always understands without needing to say much.

We first met in a TikTok live room. At first, it was just a few lighthearted comments exchanged in the chat, but gradually, we stared sharing more about our lives, our dreams, and even our pass. Despite the distance and the fact that we have never met in person, I always feel like she is right there beside me, listening and understanding.

From that relationship, I started thinking more about friendship in modern society. In this era of rapidly developing technology, many people worry that we are becoming more lonely and superficial with each other. However, I believe that the online world is also a place that connects kindred spirits, people who seem like strangers but understand each other better than anyone else. The issue isn’t about meeting face-to-face or geographical distance, but about the sincerity in how we listen and share with each other.

Modern friendships aren’t limited by distance or time anymore. They can start from a simple message, a clumsy emoji, or even a shared comment on a post. But the core remains unchanged: eal friendship is still about two people who truly listen and understand each other.

That said, we also need to be aware that not every online relationship is genuine. There are “virtual friends” who only show up when things are good and disappear when things get tough. But if you’re lucky enough to find someone sincere, even through a screen, that’s still a beautiful memory in your life. These connections, though intangible, can leave a lasting impact and become a source of comfort and strength.

To this day, I still haven’t met her in real life. But I believe that, whether we meet or not, that friendship is real. As real as the way she appears whenever I’m at my weakest. And I also know, somewhere on the other side of the screen, there is a friend who exists, quietly but steadfastly, like a beautiful gift that modern life has given me.

Sometimes, I wonder what it would be like if we could actually meet in person. Maybe we would sit together in a cozy cafe shop, sharing stories face-to-face instead of through a screen. I imagine us laughing together with ease, and the silences would feel comfortable, just like our late night chat. Maybe we’d learn new things about one another, or just appreciate the simple joy of sharing the same space together. Even if that day never comes, the thought of it makes me smile, and reminds me that some connections are strong enough to cross any distance.
