- ##### [R for Data Science](https://r4ds.had.co.nz/introduction.html)

*R for Data Science* is one of the first books I picked up to learn data analysis properly.
It’s practical, beginner-friendly, and focuses on building a clean, consistent workflow using the tidyverse tools.
I’m using it not just to understand R, but to build a better way of thinking about data.

- ##### [R Cookbook](https://rc2e.com/)

While the first book is for those who want to learn and gain a deep understanding of data science, *R Cookbook* will be more useful once you already know R and just need specific solutions to problems in your daily work.

- #### [R Graphics Cookbook](https://r-graphics.org/)
*R Graphics Cookbook* is like having a quick fix for any plot you need in R. No heavy theory, just lots of easy to follow recipes to help you make clean, sharp visuals fast. If you already know a bit of R and just want to get your charts done without overcomplicating things, this book is a total lifesaver.

- #### [Use R! book series](https://www.springer.com/series/6991)
The *"Use R!"* series is a collection of short, practical books that cover all kinds of topics around R — from data analysis and statistics to graphics and machine learning. Each book focuses on getting you up and running with a specific topic quickly, without getting lost in too much theory.

- #### [SQL Notes for Professionals](https://goalkicker.com/SQLBook/)
*SQL Notes for Professionals* is like a cheat sheet packed with everything you need to know about SQL. It’s full of quick tips, examples, and straight to the point explanations — no long lectures, no extra stuff. Whether you’re just starting out or need a fast reminder, this book is super handy to keep around.
